# V4 Roadmap — "a quant desk for the average person" (2026-07-16)

Owner intent (Murat): the engine should act like a hedge-fund analyst for a
regular person — find candidate stocks, manage the portfolio, prove itself on
the honest paper lanes, and stay cheap enough to run for the full 24-month
record. Compete on trust (forward record) + accessibility (casual UI), not on
feature count.

Standing constraints (unchanged, non-negotiable):
- Paper-lane write path is sacred; no LLM anywhere near a trade decision
  (every leakage-controlled live test shows LLM traders lose money — see
  `docs/research/BEST_PROJECTS_SYNTHESIS_2026-07-14.md`).
- New signals require pre-registration; six forward clocks is ENOUGH — each
  new one raises the multiple-testing bar for all of them.
- No skill claims before the 24-month bar (lane-flip lesson 2026-07:
  conviction went +5.5% → −5.4% vs mirror −8.4% → +0.8% in five weeks —
  every short-window read is noise).

- **Markov chains: already load-bearing.** The 3-state HMM (a hidden Markov
  chain) drives regime detection and the regime-switching MC; BOCPD is
  Bayesian changepoint detection. Nothing new to add; documented so the ask
  isn't silently dropped.
- **Neural networks / CNNs: NO today, with a concrete GO path.** The full
  answer (asked twice, deserves one):
  1. *Sample size is the killer, and we've measured it.* Our three crash
     trials failed with LightGBM (~hundreds of parameters) because the label
     collapses to a handful of independent episodes. A neural net has
     orders of magnitude MORE parameters chasing the SAME few episodes —
     it would fail the identical pre-registered gate, slower and less
     interpretably. Training an NN on our own backtests/validations
     ("densify the trials") is fitting noise on n≈11 trials.
  2. *The best evidence says NNs buy nothing statistically significant
     over trees.* Gu-Kelly-Xiu (RFS 2020; fact-checked against the
     published tables 2026-07-16): monthly out-of-sample R² — GBRT 0.34%,
     RF 0.33%, NN3 (best net) 0.40% — and the Diebold-Mariano tests show
     the NN-vs-tree difference is NOT statistically significant. Both
     families crush linear models; the NN's nominal edge required the full
     CRSP cross-section (~30k stocks, 60 years, ~920 features) — a panel
     we don't have yet. On our data (one market, macro/price features)
     there is no evidenced NN premium to chase, and CNNs specifically
     shine where inputs have spatial structure (order books, satellite
     images; chart-image CNNs are a notorious retail data-mining trap).
     Markov structure, meanwhile, is already load-bearing here (3-state
     HMM regimes drive the MC; BOCPD changepoints).
  3. *We currently cannot validate one honestly.* T7: any NN trained on
     our survivor-only panel would print inflated numbers that DSR/PBO
     cannot catch (F-013). Shipping that would be the profit-mirage we
     reject in other people's projects.
  **GO path (pre-committed):** when the EODHD/Sharadar survivorship-free
  panel lands and passes the acceptance audit → register **TRIAL-NN-1**: a
  GKX-style feed-forward net on the delisted-inclusive cross-section,
  benchmarked against the incumbent LightGBM under purged CV + DSR/PBO,
  frozen protocol before the first fit. If the panel gives NNs the sample
  size they need, we test them the same way we test everything — and adopt
  on evidence, not fashion.
- **"Brain densified by trials" plan:** machine-readable findings ledger

  accrue → verdicts distill → the lab proposes better-informed hypotheses.

## LLM module verdict (Murat asked: replace DeepSeek?)

Keep DeepSeek for what it does today (news→structured JSON, brief summaries,
commentary): it is the cheapest capable model for bulk text→data work, the
output contract is validated JSON with template fallback, and the LLM is
deliberately OFF every trade path — so marginal quality there buys nothing
measurable. Two upgrades worth considering, in order:
1. **Task-split**: keep DeepSeek for bulk/structured extraction; route the
   few USER-FACING paragraphs/day (daily brief prose, portfolio commentary)
   to Claude Haiku 4.5 — noticeably better writing, still cents/day at our
   volume (~5-20 calls/day). `llm_analyzer` already has provider plumbing +
   spend guards.
2. **FinBERT stays** for sentiment scoring (now idle-unloaded); it is not an
   LLM cost and switching sentiment to a chat LLM would cost more and
   validate worse (FinBERT vs FPB/FiQA benchmark is a backlog item).
Do NOT: fine-tune (FinGPT LoRA rejected), or use any LLM as a stock picker.
