### Finding 1: data_generator calls `get_market_signal()` with ZERO market context
**File:** `lab/data_generator.py:247`
**Severity:** High (observability blind spot)

The data_generator calls `get_market_signal()` with no arguments at all. Every parameter defaults:
- `crash_prob_3m=None` → component zeroed + weight excluded
- `regime="Neutral"` → 0.0 (actual regime is "Bull" → should be 0.7)
- `risk_score=0.0` → macro_risk = 0.0 (actual is 1.14)
- `sp500_1m_return=0.0`, `sp500_3m_return=0.0` → momentum/mean_reversion = 0.0
- `drawdown_pct=None` → zeroed + weight excluded

This is why the engine output shows 6 of 8 signal components at 0.0. The only non-zero component (valuation=0.3) is from the default VIX=20.0 hitting the "moderate fear" bracket. **Every cycle's signal_quality measurement is essentially meaningless** — it tests the signal engine's defaults, not real market conditions.

Compare with `routers/stock.py:184-279` (`_compute_market_signal`) which correctly wires all inputs from DataFetcher, risk_scorer, regime_detector, crash model, external_validator, and drawdown computation.

### Finding 2: `stock_analyzer.py` returns keys that `data_generator.py` can't find
**File:** `lab/data_generator.py:99-101` vs `backend/services/stock_analyzer.py:259-289`

The data_generator reads:
- `data.get("crash_prob_3m")` → but stock_analyzer returns `"ml_crash_prob"` (line 280). **Key mismatch** → always null.
- `data.get("signal", {}).get("action")` → stock_analyzer returns NO `signal` dict at all. Signals are computed at the router level (`routers/stock.py:338-356`), not in the service. **Structural gap** → always null.

This means the `crash_prob_3m: null` and `signal_action: null` in every cycle's stock_analysis output are caused by the data_generator looking for fields that don't exist in the service output.
