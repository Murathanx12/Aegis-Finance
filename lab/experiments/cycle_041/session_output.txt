**What I built:** A prediction confidence scoring system that quantifies uncertainty in stock analysis predictions.

**The problem:** The system detects 89.2% critical feature drift, but stock analysis returns point estimates with zero uncertainty metadata. Users see precise-looking numbers with no indication of reliability.

**The solution:**
- **`prediction_confidence.py`** — 5-component confidence scorer:
  - **Drift severity** (35% weight) — the dominant uncertainty source right now
  - **MC spread** (20%) — how wide P10-P90 is relative to median  
  - **GARCH tail quality** (15%) — fat tails = harder to predict
  - **Data sufficiency** (15%) — more history = better calibration
  - **Beta stability** (15%) — extreme betas are harder to project

- Outputs: **Grade A-F**, numeric score 0-1, drift-adjusted P10/P90 intervals
- Under critical drift, intervals **widen 60%** (e.g., P10 drops further, P90 rises further)
