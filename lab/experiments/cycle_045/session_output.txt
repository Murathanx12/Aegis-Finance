### What I built
The signal engine now adapts its component weights based on the detected market regime:

| Component | Default | Bull | Bear | Volatile |
|-----------|---------|------|------|----------|
| **momentum** | 0.12 | **0.20** | 0.06 | 0.08 |
| **crash_prob** | 0.20 | 0.14 | **0.24** | 0.18 |
| **mean_reversion** | 0.09 | 0.05 | **0.16** | **0.14** |
| **valuation** | 0.11 | 0.08 | 0.12 | **0.16** |
| **drawdown** | 0.10 | **0.15** | 0.06 | 0.08 |

### Why it matters
- **Bull markets**: Momentum is the strongest factor (Jegadeesh & Titman). Drawdown confirms trend proximity to highs.
- **Bear markets**: Crash risk and mean reversion dominate. Momentum breaks down in downtrends.
- **Volatile markets**: VIX-based valuation signals are most informative. Momentum is unreliable in whipsaws.
- Drift adjustment layers on top of regime weights (not conflicting).
