## What I changed

**Problem:** `STOCK_CAGR_CAPS` were hardcoded in `stock_analyzer.py` (violating project rules) with overly aggressive caps — mega-cap stocks were limited to 15% annual drift, meaning NVDA (63% historical), AAPL (28%), and all other mega-caps showed identical 15% projections. This destroyed signal differentiation in the screener.

**Fix: Bayesian drift shrinkage** (2 files changed)

1. **`backend/config.py`** — Added `stocks.cagr_caps` (widened: mega 15%→30%, large 20%→35%) and `stocks.drift_shrinkage` parameters (prior=7% equity premium, shrinkage adapts to data length)

2. **`backend/services/stock_analyzer.py`** — Replaced hard `np.clip()` with shrinkage formula: `weight * prior + (1-weight) * hist_mu`, where weight decreases from 60% (1yr data) to 25% (5yr data)
