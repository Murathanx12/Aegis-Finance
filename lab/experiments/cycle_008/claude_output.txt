Done. Here's a summary of Cycle 8:

**Problem found:** `stock_analyzer.py` had two related bugs:
1. **Unit inconsistency** — `hist_mu` (log drift) was blended with `prior` (0.07 arithmetic) and analyst targets (arithmetic) without converting to consistent units
2. **Missing Ito correction** — the blended result was passed directly to `simulate_paths` as a log drift without applying `-0.5*sigma^2`, which `run_monte_carlo` correctly does (fixed in cycle 7)

**Impact:** High-vol stocks had systematically inflated MC projections by ~0.5*sigma^2/year (NVDA: +12%, TSLA: +10%, AAPL: +4%)

**Fix:** Convert all drift components to arithmetic return space before blending, then apply `log(1+r) - 0.5*sigma^2` to get the proper log drift for `simulate_paths`.

**Results:**

|--------|--------|-------|
| Mean drift error | 31.1% | 27.1% (-13%) |
